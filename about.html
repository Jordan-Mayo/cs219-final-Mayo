Researcher & Educator

Bridging the gap between theoretical research and human-centric design.

I am a Senior Research Scientist specializing in Cognitive Psychology and Human-Computer Interaction. My work explores how digital environments influence attention and decision-making in high-stakes professional settings.

Background

With over a decade of experience in both academia and industry, I have dedicated my career to understanding the symbiotic relationship between humans and the tools they create. My doctoral thesis at the University of Oxford investigated neuroplasticity in the age of rapid information consumption.

Since then, I have balanced my time between leading the Cognitive Design Lab and consulting for Fortune 500 tech firms on ethical UX practices. I believe that professional credibility is built on rigorous data, but accessibility is what makes that data impactful.

Areas of Expertise

Cognitive Science

Analyzing mental processes such as perception, memory, and reasoning to better understand how users interact with complex information systems.

HCI Research

Bridging computer science and behavioral science to design interfaces that minimize cognitive load and maximize user agency.

Academic Writing

Translating complex research findings into peer-reviewed publications and accessible white papers.

Cross-Functional Leadership

Coordinating research efforts between engineering teams, design departments, and academic stakeholders to ensure evidence-based product development.
